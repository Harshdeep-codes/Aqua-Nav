About Us

Our mission is to empower communities and governmental bodies in their quest to provide clean and sustainable drinking water to every rural household. With a deep commitment to the Jal Jeevan Mission, we have developed an innovative web-based tool that integrates mapping, geospatial database management, grievance redressal, and IoT monitoring. Our dedicated team of experts is driven by a shared vision of ensuring access to clean water for all, and we are proud to contribute to this vital mission.

  • Universal Access

    Our goal is to support sustainable and efficient water supply networks that endure for generations.

  • Empowerment

    We aim to empower communities and authorities with the tools they need to manage water resources effectively.

Mapping and Geospatial Database Management

  • focuses on creating and maintaining a comprehensive geospatial database of water supply infrastructure, including pipelines, sources, treatment plants, and distribution points.
  • It enables accurate visualization, monitoring, and management of the network's physical components using GIS technology.
  • The mapping and geospatial data management pillar ensures that decision-makers have access to comprehensive, location-based information for effective planning, monitoring, and maintenance of the network.

IoT Monitoring and Data Analytics

  • The IoT (Internet of Things) pillar involves deploying sensors and devices to collect real-time data on water flow rates, pressure, and quality indicators.
  • This data is analyzed to optimize operations, predict maintenance needs, and ensure the continuous and efficient delivery of clean water.

Grievance Redressal and Transparency

  • This pillar emphasizes community engagement and transparency. It includes a grievance redressal system that allows users to report water supply issues or concerns.
  • The system tracks and resolves reported grievances transparently, and a public-facing interface enables users to monitor the status of their complaints.

Capacity Building and Stakeholder Engagement

  • Capacity building involves training and empowering local communities and authorities to actively participate in the sustainable management of water supply networks.
  • Stakeholder engagement involves collaboration with government agencies, NGOs, technology partners, and local communities to ensure collective efforts in achieving the mission's goals.
